About

Hello, my name is Paolo Vincent, I’m 18 years old and I’m currently in my first year of MMI at Montbéliard (the guy with the pink t-shirt).

This website is my response to the topic of the first semester project: Prehistory of the Future.
I chose to talk about transhumanism and especially how people from a distant future, living in a world where transhumanity would be the norm, might perceive us.

I think this theme is relevant because I imagine that we, simple humans made of muscles and bones, will seem very primitive, very prehistoric, to future archaeologists.

Moreover, the accelerationist trend dominating today's tech fields, especially AI, raises questions about the ethics of these innovations, reflections that can be explored on this site.

Finally, the idea of making ArchEVE a site for collaborative writing allows everyone to position themselves as a future archaeologist to develop and share their opinions and thoughts on our world, as well as on the world of tomorrow.

If you have any questions or would like to contact me for anything, feel free to do so via the form available on the site.
