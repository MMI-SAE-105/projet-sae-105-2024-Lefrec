The Evolution of Neuralink

By the ArchEVE team
Archive from 21/10/2634

2016 - Foundation and First Prototypes

The first prototypes of Neuralink, developed in the lab, resemble flat modules the size of a coin, with thin cables to capture neural signals. Used on animals, these prototypes are external devices designed to read brain activity and test electrode technology without damaging tissues.

2024 - First Human Implantation

Neuralink implants its device for the first time in a human, using a small rounded titanium disk with filaments inserted into precise areas of the brain. The implant, fully subcutaneous, is discreet and designed for medical treatments, especially to restore mobility in people paralyzed through neural stimulation.

2035 - Neuralink Mini V2

The implant becomes smaller and lighter, taking the shape of a thin disk almost invisible under the skin. Now equipped with a wireless rechargeable battery, it is permanently implanted with a minimal incision. It offers advanced features like cognitive stimulation and Bluetooth connectivity with external devices.

2075 - Neuralink NEXUS

Neuralink evolves into a network of transparent micro-capsules linked by micro-light filaments, installed by surgical nanobots. This modular device, capable of automatic adaptation, integrates an AI to assist brain functions and allows direct connection to complex virtual environments.

2250 - Neuralink Holo-Cortex

The implant transforms into a holographic network integrated into the cerebral cortex, visible only through infrared scans. The surgical nanobots build this device, which interacts with the entire nervous system without visible material components. This evolution enables the exploration of shared consciousness states and immersive augmented realities.
