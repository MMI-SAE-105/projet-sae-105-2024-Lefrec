Glossary

Android

A robot designed to resemble and act like a human being. The concept of androids raises questions about consciousness and the boundary between machines and humanity, particularly in works of science fiction such as Blade Runner and Philip K. Dick's Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?.

Cyberpunk

A subgenre of science fiction that depicts futuristic societies marked by advanced technology, often at the expense of human values. Ghost in the Shell is an iconic example, with its themes of brain-computer interface and its reflections on identity and technology.

Daft Punk

A French electronic music duo known for exploring technology and human identity through music. Their track Infinity Repeating, released in 2023, reflects on the endless technological cycle and human-machine fusion, marking the end of their musical career and a meditation on the transhumanist future.

Denis Villeneuve

A Canadian director famous for his science fiction films, such as Blade Runner 2049 and the Dune trilogy. His work explores themes of machine consciousness, augmented humanity, and our place in the universe. He is recognized for enriching the discourse on transhumanism and space exploration in the early 21st century.

Dune

A film series directed by Denis Villeneuve, based on the novels by Frank Herbert. This trilogy, which began in 2021, addresses issues of power, ecology, and human evolution through space exploration. It has had a significant impact on transhumanist thought and the question of humanity's survival beyond Earth.

Ghost in the Shell

A Japanese animated film directed by Mamoru Oshii in 1995, based on the manga by Masamune Shirow. It explores themes of identity and human-machine fusion in a cyberpunk world. The work critiques the consequences of invasive technology on society and reflects on potential dehumanization.

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, particularly computer systems. AI is a central subject in many works of science fiction, raising questions about consciousness and the limits of technology. The films of Denis Villeneuve and the works of Philip K. Dick are often cited for their philosophical explorations of AI.

Neuralink

A company founded by Elon Musk in the early 21st century, dedicated to the development of brain-machine interfaces. Initially intended to treat certain neurological disorders, Neuralink's technology is often seen as a milestone toward transhumanism, raising ethical questions about human-machine fusion.

Philip K. Dick

An American science fiction author known for works exploring the nature of reality and humanity. His novel Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep? inspired many debates on machine consciousness and the definition of humanity, influencing contemporary transhumanist works.

Science Fiction

A literary and cinematic genre that explores the consequences of technology on society and human nature. Works of science fiction, such as those by Denis Villeneuve or Philip K. Dick, raise questions about humanity's evolution, space exploration, and machine consciousness, marking the beginning of transhumanist reflections.

Transhumanism

A philosophical and cultural movement aimed at improving the human condition through technology, biology, and the sciences. Transhumanism addresses human-machine fusion, space exploration, and the evolution of human abilities. Works such as Blade Runner, Ghost in the Shell, and Denis Villeneuve's films are often cited for their exploration of these concepts.
