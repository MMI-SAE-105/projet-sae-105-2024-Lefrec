Glossary

Android

A robot designed to resemble and act like a human being. The concept of an android raises questions about consciousness and the boundary between machine and humanity, particularly in science-fiction works such as *Blade Runner* and *Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?* by Philip K. Dick.

Cyberpunk

A subgenre of science fiction depicting futuristic societies shaped by advanced technology, often at the cost of human values. Ghost in the Shell is a prime example, with its themes of brain-machine interfaces and reflections on identity and technology.

Daft Punk

A French electronic music duo known for their explorations of technology and human identity through music. Their track *Infinity Repeating*, released in 2023, reflects on the endless technological cycle and human-machine fusion, marking the end of their musical career and meditating on a transhumanist future.

Denis Villeneuve

A Canadian filmmaker renowned for his science fiction films, such as Blade Runner 2049 and the *Dune* trilogy. His work delves into themes of machine consciousness, augmented humanity, and our place in the universe, contributing to transhumanist reflections and space exploration.

Dune

A series of films directed by Denis Villeneuve, based on novels by Frank Herbert. This trilogy, which began in 2021, tackles issues of power, ecology, and human evolution through space conquest, having a major impact on transhumanist thought and humanity's survival beyond Earth.
